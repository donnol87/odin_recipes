Where did the Pale Ale come from?

Pale ale is a typically golden to amber coloured style of ale which is brewed using an ale yeast and predominantly pale malt.[1][2][3] The term first appeared around 1703 for beers made from malts dried with high-carbon coke, which resulted in a lighter colour than other beers popular at that time. Different brewing practices and hop quantities have resulted in a range of tastes and strengths within the pale ale family.

How to make a pale ale

  1. Steep the Barley
  2. Boil the Wort
  3. Cool the Wort
  4. Add the yeast
  5. Transfter to primary fermenter and leave for 2 weeks
  6. Tranfser to secondary fermenter and leave for 1 week
  7. Bottle and leave for 1 week
  8. Refrigirate for 6 hours and ENJOY!!
